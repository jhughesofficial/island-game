# Pricing Notes

## Recommendation: $2.99

### Rationale

**$2.99 is the correct price for this game at launch.** Here's why:

**1. Length and scope.**
The Island is a session game. A first playthrough to the breaking news reveal takes 30–60 minutes of active play (longer with idle time). Three endings and Ghost Mode add replayability, but the core experience is compact. Pricing above $3.99 risks review backlash from players who finish Act 3 and feel the runtime didn't justify the cost. At $2.99, you're priced against the runtime honestly.

**2. Impulse-buy threshold.**
Steam's lowest viable price tier is $0.99 (which signals shovelware) and $1.99 (which signals asset flip). $2.99 is the sweet spot: low enough to be an impulse buy for anyone mildly curious, high enough to signal intentionality. The game's satirical angle means word-of-mouth is likely to drive more units than ads — low price amplifies that flywheel.

**3. Web version is free.**
If there's a web/itch.io release, pricing needs to justify the Steam premium. $2.99 is defensible as "the version with achievements, cloud saves, and the full narrative experience." Anything above $4.99 with a free web version in circulation will generate negative sentiment.

**4. Controversy upside.**
This game may attract attention from people who aren't sure how they feel about it. A low price reduces the cost of "trying it to form an opinion." Some of the best coverage this game could receive comes from people who bought it half-expecting to be outraged and ended up thinking about it for a week. Don't price them out.

## Launch Discount Strategy

| Window | Price | Discount |
|---|---|---|
| Launch (week 1) | $1.99 | 33% off |
| Post-launch standard | $2.99 | — |
| Steam sale events | $1.49 | 50% off |
| Deep sale / bundle floor | $0.99 | 67% off |

**Launch discount rationale:** A 33% day-one discount ($1.99) maximizes wishlist-to-purchase conversion on launch day and helps with Steam's algorithm — initial sales velocity affects placement in "New and Trending." The game's controversial hook makes early reviews important; getting volume quickly means the review score stabilizes before it matters most.

## Bundle / Regional Considerations

- **Regional pricing:** Use Steam's recommended regional pricing ratios. Don't over-adjust; the game's subject matter resonates internationally.
- **Bundle candidacy:** Could fit well in a "dark games" or "political satire" bundle on Humble or Fanatical after 6 months. Floor price for bundle inclusion: $0.99.
- **itch.io:** Suggest pay-what-you-want with $1.99 suggested price, free minimum. This lets the curious in and captures goodwill from the indie/itch audience.
