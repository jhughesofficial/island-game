# Steam Store Description

Build the most exclusive private island retreat in the world. Manage venues, recruit VIP guests, and grow your empire — until your numbers start telling a different story.

## Long Description

**A Private Resort Management Sim. Or so it seems.**

Welcome to The Island — a secluded paradise where old money meets new power and the guest list is very, very exclusive. Click your way from a humble bonfire on the beach to a sprawling compound with media suites, private airstrips, and the kind of Political Influence that keeps the heat off. Build it up. Automate it. Watch the numbers climb.

It plays exactly like an idle game. That's the point.

**Idle mechanics with a dark satirical core.** Upgrade 8 distinct venues, hire staff who work for you around the clock, and spend your wealth on multipliers that turn thousands into millions. Eight VIP guests cycle through your operation, each one boosting your income — and your exposure. A five-star Heat meter watches everything you do. Let it spike and the feds come knocking. Manage it carefully, and you might just retire clean.

**The story arrives whether you're paying attention or not.** Eight narrative events surface as your empire grows, reframing what you thought you were building. A live news ticker evolves across three acts — first buzzing with society-page gossip, then sharpening into something more uncomfortable, then breaking entirely. By the time Act 3 hits, you'll understand what $50 million in this business actually means.

**Three ways to finish. One truth.** Get arrested when the walls close in. Escape before they do. Or cash out so deep into legitimacy that nobody can touch you. Each ending lands differently. The Retired ending unlocks Ghost Mode — a New Game+ that layers new mechanics and lets you ask what you'd do a second time, knowing everything.

**Replayability is baked in.** Fifteen achievements, a prestige system, Secrets hidden in the idle loop, and a Statistics panel that quietly logs everything. The longer you play, the more the game says about why you kept playing.

> Three ways out. One truth.
